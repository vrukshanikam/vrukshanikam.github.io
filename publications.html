Long Short-Term Memory Implementation Exploiting Passive RRAM Crossbar Array

Honey Nikam, Siddharth Satyam, Shubham Sahay
IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices

We propose an extremely area- and energy-efficient LSTM network implementation exploiting the passive resistive random access memory (RRAM) crossbar array. We developed a hardware-aware LSTM network simulation framework and performed an extensive analysis of the proposed LSTM implementation considering the non-ideal hardware artifacts such as spatial (device-to-device) and temporal variations, non-linearity, noise, etc. utilizing an experimentally calibrated comprehensive phenomenological model for passive RRAM crossbar array. Our results indicate that the proposed passive RRAM crossbar-based LSTM network implementation not only outperforms the prior digital and active 1T-1R crossbar-based LSTM implementations by more than three orders of magnitude in terms of area and two orders of magnitude in terms of training energy for identical network accuracy, but also exhibits robustness against spatial and temporal variations and noise, and a faster convergence rate.

Energy-Efficient Implementation of Generative Adversarial Networks on Passive RRAM Crossbar Arrays

Siddharth Satyam, Honey Nikam, Shubham Sahay
Under Review

In this work, we have proposed an implementation of Generative Adversarial Netwtoks on passive memristor crossbar arrays. We have performed a fixed amplitude training to update the weights during training. We also compared the accuracy and energy efficiency of the proposed implementation using true random noise as the input. The simulation results show that our implementation has low energy consumption with comparable accuracies to the software counterpart.
